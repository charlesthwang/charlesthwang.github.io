Simpler Appointment Scheduling for Blood Donations

Overview

This project is a redesign of the appointment scheduling process on the Red Cross Blood Donor app.

My goal was to design a new interface and flow that allowed users to quickly and confidently schedule blood donation appointments.

Challenge

I recently donated blood for the first time, but had a difficult time trying to schedule an appointment through the Red Cross Blood Donor app.

It was frustrating that the app continually displayed appointment dates that didn't have any open time slots, but the process flow also felt inefficient.

My challenge was to simplify the scheduling flow, while also updating the UI to feel more like Material Design.

Validating the Problem

I wanted to know if I was the only one who struggled to make an appointment on the app, so I dove in to the app reviews on both the App Store and the Play Store.

The app seemed to have other technical issues, but there were many people who also expressed frustration scheduling an appointment. Many deserted the app altogether to schedule an appointment over the phone or on the website.

Empathy Mapping & Persona Creation

To better visualize the thoughts expressed in the user reviews, I put together an empathy map and created a proto-persona to guide me in making my design decisions.

User Need Statement

Gabe, a volunteer firefighter with an irregular schedule, needs to easily find open time slots to donate blood, in order to confidently book appointments and save time.

Heuristic Analysis

To pinpoint where users might be getting frustrated, I created a task flow of the current app.

Choosing a Date

Users are limited to the current date or a strict 14-day date range. They aren't able to choose a future date or date range shorter than 14 days.

Choosing a Time

The app doesn't respect user input here (except for "Anytime"). In my use, the search results weren't restricted to my preferred time of day.

Choosing a Donation Type

Multiple users stated they wished they could search for multiple donation types. The current app forces users to reset the search results when they change the donation type.

Location and Search Results

This was the most frustrating stage of the scheduling flow. The app doesn't let the user choose a specific donation center and just lists all centers near them. And so, because the search results combines both the date and location, the resulting list is unwieldy.

Ideation

Brainstorming for the new design, I took inspiration from various booking, appointment, and calendar mockups I found on the web. I also studied the scheduling process on American Red Cross's website and Google's Material Design documentation to inform my final designs.

Final Design

New Task Flow

I based my design around the assumption that users most value being able to search open time slots at a donation center of their choosing. This generates search results that are much more managable and easier to act on.

Choosing a Date

Users can quickly select a date up to a week out from the current date, or with the date range picker, choose a date further out or select a date range. The date range picker also provides a much clearer visualization of their selection.

Choosing a Location

The new design allows users to explicitly select a donation center. By limiting search results to a single location, users can focus better on the dates and times that works for them.

Users can also set a donation center as their "home location", allowing them to search times at their most preferred center by default.

Creating your Appointment

After selecting a date and donation center, users can choose from open times to create an appointment (if a date range was entered, users are first presented with dates that have availability).

The biggest change I made here was to combine selecting the time and donation type into one task. Users can see all open times and the donation types available at those times.

This eliminates the step of users currently having to choose a time of day (morning, afternoon, evening) and solves their need to search for more than donation type at a time.

Reflection

I learned a lot working through this project. Although I was unable to perform usuability testing (due to social distancing measures), I was able to distill the information gathered from app reviews into a clear user archetype that helped keep my process centered around user needs. This project was also a great exercise in taking complicated user flows and and interactions and finding ways to simplify and streamline them.

Although my design choices were based on user feedback, they still involved some trade-offs. My search results are limited to one location at a time, which could be too restricting for some users. There's always room for improvement, but hopefully this concept could help people find more opportunities to donate.
